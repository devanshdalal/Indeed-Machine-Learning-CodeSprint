HUMAN RESOURCES GENERALIST JOB DESCRIPTION  Mission:  The Human Resources Generalist reports the Director of Human Resources of Azura Living in Colorado. The Human Resources Generalist will guide and manage the overall provision of Human Resources services, policies, and programs for the thirteen Azura Memory Care facilities and the Corporate Office in Wisconsin. This position will promote Azura’s Core Values: Respect – We treat all guests and co-workers with the utmost respect. Recover – Help our guests recover physically, mentally, and spiritually. Recruit and Retain – We recruit and retain the finest individuals. Innovate - We lead by innovation and creativity. Always Be Exceptional. The five strengths and competencies held by the candidate for this position are: 1. Recruiting experience. 2. Employee Satisfaction – Benefits, Data Collection and Use of the Data. 3. Employee Review Management 4. Labor Law & Employee Relations 5. Experience building positive cultures and reducing turnover.  The major areas of responsibility are:   • recruiting and staffing;  • performance management and improvement systems;  • organization development;  • employment and compliance to regulatory concerns;  • team member orientation, development, and training;  • policy development and documentation;  • team member relations;  • company-wide committee facilitation;  • company team member and community communication;  • compensation and benefits administration;  • team member safety, welfare, wellness and health;  • team member services and counseling.   • The Human Resources Generalist will originate and lead Human Resources practices and objectives that will provide a team member-oriented, high performance culture that emphasizes our core values. The Human Resources Generalist serves on the executive management team; and assists and advises company managers about Human Resources issues. Primary Objectives:   • Safety of the workforce.  • Development of a positive culture and well trained workforce.  • Development of the Human Resources department,  • Expertise in turnover reduction.  • Development of a team member-oriented company culture that emphasizes quality, continuous improvement, and high performance.  • Personal ongoing development.   • Specific Requirements:  • Must hold at least a bachelor’s degree preferably in human resources or a related field.  • Must have the ability to maintain confidentiality and exercise extreme discretion in all business matters.  • Must have the ability to deal tactfully with guests, family members, visitors, government agencies/personnel and the general public.  • Prefer certification by SHRM.
